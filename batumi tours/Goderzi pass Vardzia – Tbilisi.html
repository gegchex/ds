Batumi Goderzi pass–Vardzia–Tbilisi

Jeep Tour

General Tour Description:
Join us on a captivating two-day adventure through enchanting landscapes and historic sites of Southern Georgia! Begin your journey in the stunning Acharian Mountains, where sweeping views and lush greenery surround you. Visit the charming Château Iveri for lunch, where the warm hospitality and rich flavors of Georgian culture await you. Continue your day with the exploration of Rabati Fortress with its centuries-old blend of culture and architecture. Stay overnight in a hotel within the ancient walls of Rabati Fortress and enjoy delicious dinner there. Next day, explore incredible cave city - Vardzia, a cliffside wonder with hundreds of caves carved into the rock. Then, unwind at the peaceful Paravani and Sagamo Lakes, where nature’s beauty creates a perfect retreat. This tour is a journey through history, nature, and culinary delights—promising unforgettable memories at every turn!
  • Day 1
  • Departure from Batumi;
  • Visit Makhuntseti waterfall;

    Makhuntseti Waterfall, nestled in the scenic gorge, offers an unforgettable experience for nature lovers and adventurers alike. Located just above the charming village of Makhuntseti, at 335 meters above sea level, this breathtaking 50-meter-high water cascade tumbles into a picturesque pool, forming a mesmerizing whirlpool below. The waterfall is framed by striking natural rock formations. Visitors can enjoy the beauty of the falls from nearby restaurants or unwind in peaceful picnic spots that invite relaxation in the heart of nature. Whether you're seeking a tranquil escape or a place to marvel at Georgia's natural wonders, Makhuntseti Waterfall is sure to captivate your senses.

  • Lunch in Château Iveri (on request);

    Château Iveri is more than just a destination—it offers an experience that blends culinary, culture, and the beauty of Georgia’s countryside. Surrounded by lush landscapes, this elegant estate invites visitors to explore the warm hospitality that defines Georgian culture. Learn the secrets of its ancient culinary traditions, taste the country’s renowned wines, and unwind in an atmosphere of pure relaxation and refinement. Whether you're strolling through the serene gardens or savoring a delicious dish, every moment at Château Iveri encourages you to slow down, take a deep breath, and fully immerse yourself in the experience.

  • Visit Begleti Mosque (on request);

    Over 300 years old, Beghleti Mosque is one of the oldest and important landmarks in Georgia. Originally built in 1650-1700, the mosque was restored in XIX c. after the first structure burned down. Though it served as a warehouse during the Soviet era, its rich history and unique artistry have still survived. The mosque is decorated with the vibrant wall paintings, featuring motifs like folded scarves, lemon trees, and golden floral bouquets. The stunning blue and pink bubble-shaped designs and the intricate wood carvings on the ceiling medallion showcase a blend of local and Islamic craftsmanship. In 2006, Beghleti Mosque was officially recognized as a cultural monument of national importance where you can explore enduring elegance of Georgian architecture and artistry.

  • Visit Rabat Fortress in Akhaltsikhe;

    Rabati Fortress, originally known as "Lomsia Castle," was built in the 9th century in Akhaltsikhe. The castle complex, spanning 8 hectares, showcases buildings from the 9th to 19th centuries, reflecting the fortress’s long and varied history. Initially rebuilt and expanded in the 12th century, the fortress evolved into a city and was renamed "Akhaltsikhe." By the 13th century, it became the residence of the Jakeli family and the administrative hub of Meskheti. Recently renovated Rabati blends elements of Georgian, Ottoman, Russian, Hebrew, and Armenian architecture, highlighting the region's historical cultural diversity. The old district of Akhaltsikhe is notable for its coexistence of various religious buildings, including a Georgian Orthodox Church, Mosque, Catholic Monastery, Synagogue and Armenian Church.

  • Accommodation and dinner in Hotel Gino Wellness in Rabati Fortress.

    Nested within the walls of Rabati Fortress, this charming hotel offers a unique blend of history and comfort. Just steps away from the fascinating Samtskhe–Javakheti History Museum and a short walk from the center of Akhaltsikhe, the hotel features a cozy restaurant and bar with rustic brick walls, a terrace lounge perfect for unwinding. Guests can start their day with a complimentary breakfast; later, enjoy a spa, featuring soothing whirlpool tubs and saunas. In Gino Wellness Rabat, every moment is designed for relaxation and enjoyment.

  • Day 2
  • Departure from Akhaltsikhe;
  • Visit cave city – Vardzia;

    Vardzia - monumental project from the 12th century - is a breathtaking underground city carved directly into the cliffs of southern Erusheti mountains. Conceived as both secret cave city and a monastic center it stretches over 500 meters along the slopes, with 400 rooms, 25 wine cellars, several cave churches, conference halls, bakeries, refectories and even pharmacy. The inhabitants of the city lived in rock-hewn dwellings ranging over 13 floors, a scale almost impossible to imagine at the time, tunnels, chapels, and halls connected by a labyrinth of tunnel passageways.

  • Lunch close to Vardzia (on request);
  • Photo Stop at Paravani and Sagamo lakes;

    Paravani is the largest lake in Georgia, located amidst the stunning volcanic mountains of Javakheti. At 2,073 meters above sea level, this tranquil lake spans a vast surface area and reaches depths of up to 3.3 meters. In the winter months, Paravani Lake transforms into a frozen wonderland, with ice reaching a thickness of 47–73 cm, creating an impressive sight. Fed by snow, rain, and underground springs, the lake is a haven for nature lovers and a popular spot for fishing.
    Saghamo Lake, situated at 1,996 meters above sea level, offers tranquil scenery surrounded by volcanic hills. The lake sits in a basin with a maximum depth of 2.3 meters. The Paravan River flowing through, connects Saghamo Lake to nearby Paravani Lake. Fed by rain, snow, and underground springs, this tectonic-origin lake boasts a shelter for those seeking a serene nature.
