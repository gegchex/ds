Machakhela National Park,
Masterclass at Acharian Gunsmith’s

General Tour Description:
Embark on an unforgettable journey through the breathtaking landscapes and rich heritage of the Machakhela Valley! Your adventure begins at the stunning Mirveti Waterfall, where crystal waters cascade down the cliffs in a mesmerizing display of nature’s beauty. Then, step back in time as you explore the ancient Gvara Fortress and cross the 13th-century Tskhemlari Bridge. At the Ethnographic Museum, immerse yourself in the local culture before stepping into the Machakhela Gunsmith Shop for a unique masterclass in crafting traditional bullets. Cap off your day with an unforgettable feast at Emzar’s family restaurant, where you’ll savor authentic Georgian cuisine while being entertained by the vibrant dances and songs performed by the family. This tour offers the perfect mix of adventure, history, and culture, making it a truly unforgettable experience!
  • Departure from Batumi;
  • Visit Mirveti Waterfall;

    Nestled just 27 km from Batumi, the charming village of Mirveti offers a hidden gem for nature lovers and adventurers alike. Situated on the left bank of the Chorokhi River, at an altitude of 60 meters, Mirveti is home to a breathtaking waterfall that cascades 20 meters down the cliffs, filling the air with its refreshing roar. What makes the journey to this natural wonder even more special is the enchanting path that leads you there through the alley of ancient Kolkhetian Boxwoods—species so rare they are protected in the Red Book. Mirveti Waterfall isn’t just a destination, it’s an experience. Whether you’re looking to escape the bustle of the city or simply reconnect with nature.

  • Visit Gvara Fortress;

    Perched at the confluence of the Chorokhi and Machakhela rivers, Gvara Fortress offers a glimpse into Georgia's ancient past. Standing 50 meters above the Machakhela River, this pre-feudal fortress once controlled key trade routes. Its towering walls, now standing 3-6 meters tall, still bear witness to its role as a watchful guardian of the region. Although much about Gvara Fortress remains shrouded in centuries, the allure of its untold history and the breathtaking scenery surrounding it make the site truly captivating. Its hidden past invites exploration, and the sense of secrecy adds to its charm, offering visitors a site where history and nature intertwine.

  • Photo stop at Tskhemlara Bridge;

    The Tskhemlara Arch Bridge, built in the middle ages, stands as a stunning testament to Georgia's architectural heritage. This 22-meter-long bridge spans the Machakhela River, offering a glimpse into the region’s past. Its unique masonry and narrow design set it apart from other arched bridges, giving it a distinctive charm. Declared a cultural monument of national importance in 2006 and carefully restored in 2008, this ancient bridge invites visitors to step back in time and admire the craftsmanship that has stood for centuries.

  • Visit Ethnographic Museum of Machakhela;

    Museim is located in a beautifully preserved 18th-century former mosque and it offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and artistry. The intricate wood carvings, crafted by Laz craftman, still dazzle with vibrant colors that have withstood the test of time, making the building itself a work of art. Inside, the museum showcases a rich collection of artifacts that bring the region’s past to life. You’ll find archaeological discoveries, traditional tools, and fascinating ethnographic items that offer a glimpse into the daily lives of those who once lived in the valley. Open every day except weekends, from 10:00 am to 6:00 pm, the Ethnographic Museum invites you to step into a world where culture, history, and craftsmanship come together.

  • Masterclass at Machakhela Gunsmith’s Shop and Museum;

    In the heart of the Machakhela Valley, rich tradition of Georgian craftsmanship comes to life. In the 17th century, skilled masters in this valley were renowned for creating special rifles in their forges, where they melted steel and meticulously crafted wooden butts to make these extraordinary weapons. Today, centuries later, this proud legacy continues. Master craftsman Zaza Nagervadze has recreated the iconic Machakhela Gun using the same time-honored techniques. At the museum, you can not only admire this stunning replica but also immerse yourself in history by participating in a hands-on master class. Experience the art of crafting a gun bullet, guided by expert artisans. This journey into past is an experience for history enthusiasts and curious travelers alike, offering a rare chance to engage with the heritage of Machakhela in an unforgettable way.

  • Lunch at Emzar's culinary & folklore.

    Step into the warm embrace of a local family-owned restaurant, where Georgian culinary traditions and vibrant folklore come together to create an unforgettable experience. Renowned for its authentic Acharian cuisine, this hidden gem invites you to savor the delicious masterpieces prepared by locals, using time-honored regional recipes. And it’s more than just a meal—it’s a feast for all the senses. As you enjoy the rich flavors of Georgian dishes, you’ll be captivated by live performances of traditional dances and songs, lovingly presented by restaurant owner - Emzar, his wife, and their children. The lively rhythms, heartfelt melodies, and family atmosphere will leave you feeling like part of the family.

  • Return to Batumi.
